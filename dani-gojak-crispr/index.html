How CRISPR edits DNA

CRISPR uses a guide RNA and the Cas9 enzyme to find, cut, and repair a chosen DNA site.

Scientists first choose a target DNA sequence inside a gene region.

Then guide RNA and Cas9 work together to find and cut that exact spot.

After the cut, the cell repairs the DNA, which is where editing can happen.

Focus labels: gene region, base pairs, target DNA sequence, edited site.

Step 1

Gene region

A gene is a section of DNA that contains instructions used by the cell.

This larger label shows the DNA region where the target site is located.

CRISPR is aimed at one chosen part of the DNA, not the entire strand at once.

Step 2

Target DNA

Inside that region, scientists choose a short DNA sequence to edit.

The brackets mark the exact sequence the CRISPR system will search for.

Picking the correct target helps reduce cutting at the wrong DNA location.

Step 3

Guide RNA binds

A guide RNA matches the target DNA sequence and guides Cas9 to that same site.

The green guide RNA acts like a molecular address label for the DNA target.

The guide RNA does not cut the DNA by itself. Its main job is to bring Cas9 to the matching sequence.

Step 4

Cas9 arrives

Cas9 is brought to the same DNA site by the guide RNA.

The Cas9 protein sits behind the DNA before the break is made.

At this stage, the guide RNA and Cas9 are working together as one targeting-and-cutting system.

Step 5

Cas9 cuts DNA

Cas9 is the enzyme that cuts both DNA strands at the target site.

The red marker shows the place where the DNA is broken.

Once Cas9 is lined up with the correct sequence, the break starts the editing process.

Step 6

DNA repair

The cell repairs the break after Cas9 has cut the DNA.

During repair, the DNA can be rejoined, disrupted, or changed at the edited site.

Scientists use this repair step either to turn a gene off or to help introduce a different DNA sequence.

Step 7

Edited DNA

After repair, the target site can end up different from the original version.

The label marks the edited DNA site after the repair step.

Use the mutation button to compare the original version with the repaired version.
