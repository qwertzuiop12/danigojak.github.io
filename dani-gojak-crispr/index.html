Activity 1 / Interactive DNA Repair Diagram

What happens after DNA is cut?

A cut in DNA is only the start. The cell then tries to repair the break.

Sometimes the repair puts the DNA back the way it was.

Sometimes the repair adds a small mistake.

That small DNA change can sometimes affect a trait.

Use the buttons to choose the repair result.

Step 1

DNA gets cut

The DNA breaks at one spot.

The red mark shows the cut in the DNA.

In this project, Cas9 is the cutting tool, but the important biology is what the cell does next.

Step 2

The cell notices

The cell detects that the DNA is broken.

Cells have repair systems because DNA damage can happen naturally too.

The goal is to close the break so the DNA strand can keep working.

Step 3

The cell tries to fix it

The cell brings the broken DNA ends back together.

This repair step is where the final result can change.

A repair can be accurate, or it can leave a tiny change in the DNA.

Step 4

Choose the repair

Pick what happens when the cell repairs the DNA.

This is the main interaction: the result depends on the repair choice.

Step 5A

Fix correctly

The DNA goes back to normal.

The instructions in the DNA stay the same.

This path shows a repair that does not change the DNA message.

Step 5B

Fix with mistake

The DNA changes a little.

A small change in DNA is called a mutation.

The change might be harmless, or it might affect how the cell reads the instructions.

Final step

This can affect traits

If DNA instructions change, a trait can sometimes change too.

Traits are features like color, shape, or how something works in the body.

The repair choice shows why the cut itself is not the whole story.
